# Future Directions

## Current State

A browser-first v1 prototype exists with title, gameplay shell, builder, modular ship assembly, quality tiers, enemy spawning, damage, and salvage attachment logic.

## Quick Wins

| Idea | Why It Matters | Likely Owner |
| --- | --- | --- |
| stronger salvage pulse and snap flash | makes the signature rebuild loop feel deliberate | Visual/Interaction Designer + Game Developer |
| first-wave pacing tune | protects the opening minutes from feeling unfair | Game Designer + Game Developer |
| clearer block-state HUD cues | helps players understand what changed after attachment or damage | Visual/Interaction Designer |

## Bigger Bets

| Idea | Why It Matters | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| boss-tier or scripted enemy frames | expands the current roster without flattening every run into the same mix | can overwhelm the current readable combat loop |
| targeted salvage hunting | makes ship upgrade choices more strategic | can slow the arcade pace |
| coordinated enemy-group behaviors | makes the archetype roster interact in more interesting ways | can hide causality and make fights harder to read |

## Deferred Questions

- Does the player need a score or wave goal beyond survival before the game feels complete?
- Should salvage stay single-cell in shipped v1, or does the fantasy demand chunkier detachments?
- How much build-time freedom is healthy before the builder overshadows the combat loop?

## Signals To Watch

- whether players understand socket compatibility without extra explanation
- whether first-session deaths feel earned or abrupt
- whether better ship builds materially change survival time and combat feel
