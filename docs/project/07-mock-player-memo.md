## First-Session Verdict

- Would I keep playing for another five minutes? Probably yes, if the drag-and-rebuild loop feels as direct in motion as it looks in the builder.
- Why or why not? The modular ship silhouette, neon readability, and salvage fantasy are clear fast. The main uncertainty is not the fantasy; it is whether the live rebuild loop feels smooth under pressure.

## What Feels Good

- The title screen establishes the tone immediately.
- The builder palette makes the block language understandable before the player even fights.
- The centered-ship gameplay presentation reads like a focused arcade prototype instead of an overbuilt sandbox.

## What Feels Bad Or Unfair

- Without a direct first-session combat play pass yet, it is hard to judge whether incoming pressure is fair or whether the player has enough time to identify and attach salvage.
- The loop will feel flat fast if enemy deaths do not create satisfying salvage moments on screen.

## Control And Responsiveness

- The browser shell suggests a clean keyboard-and-mouse control model. Real responsiveness still needs live play validation, especially around mouse drag, auto-snap, and firing while repositioning.

## Challenge And Balance

- The current direction looks appropriate for a short arcade survival loop.
- The risk is that enemy pressure lands before salvage readability does, which would make the signature mechanic feel like homework instead of power gain.

## Quick Wins

- Add a stronger salvage pickup pulse when a loose block becomes draggable.
- Add a stronger snap confirmation flash when a block attaches successfully.
- Add a clearer first enemy arrival rhythm so the opening seconds are less ambiguous.

## Bigger Bets

- Let better-quality enemy ships telegraph their loadout before the fight so players can chase specific salvage.
- Add special detached wing chunks later if the salvage fantasy needs bigger visual payoff than single-cell pieces alone.
